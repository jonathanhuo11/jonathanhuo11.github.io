What are some things this guy has done?

In terms of programming, I've built quite a few interesting doohickeys.

For about two years, I've been developing "indie" games using the Unity3D game engine. Some of my projects include a zombie shooter, a 2D physics defying version of sumotori, a matrix-style slow-motion fighting game, as well as a multiplayer ragdoll shooter.

I'm also an experienced web and systems designer. Along with the Symmidium team, I've worked on building a browser-based, distributed computation system, called DCNET. We envisioned a product that would enable researchers, students, and big companies to distribute the work of solving computationally-expensive problems across internet browsers. We were able to apply our framework to things like big data processing, solving cryptographic hashes, and computing mathematical problems.

I've also built my own programming language. During the course of three months, Ethan and I constructed a compiler and interpreter for a new language - Slang. We designed this language to be familiar to C-style programmers, have the cutting-edge paradigms seen in Swift and Rust, and yet maintain a simplicity and elegance like Python. Since it was a part of a school project, we also wrote a 40-page essay on compiler construction; bytecode optimizations; and language design.
